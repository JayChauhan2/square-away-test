CHAIN RULE AND APPLICATIONS

**Problem 1**

Original Function:
$y = \sqrt[7]{\log(\frac{1}{x})}$

Steps as written in the image:
$\implies \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{1}{7}(\log(\frac{1}{x}))^{-\frac{6}{7}} \frac{d}{dx} \log(\frac{1}{x})$
$= -\frac{1}{7}(\log(\frac{1}{x}))^{-\frac{6}{7}} \frac{d}{dx} \log x$
$= -\frac{1}{7}(\log(\frac{1}{x}))^{-\frac{6}{7}} \frac{1}{x}$

Final Result from Image:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{1}{7x (\log(\frac{1}{x}))^{\frac{6}{7}}}$

**Logical Understanding and Correction for Problem 1:**

Let $y = \sqrt[7]{\log(\frac{1}{x})}$.
We can rewrite $\log(\frac{1}{x})$ as $\log(x^{-1}) = -\log x$.
So, $y = (-\log x)^{\frac{1}{7}}$.

Using the Chain Rule, $\frac{d}{dx}(u^n) = n u^{n-1} \frac{du}{dx}$:
Let $u = -\log x$ and $n = \frac{1}{7}$.
$\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(-\log x) = -\frac{1}{x}$.

Now substitute into the chain rule formula:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{7}(-\log x)^{\frac{1}{7}-1} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}(-\log x)$
$= \frac{1}{7}(-\log x)^{-\frac{6}{7}} \cdot (-\frac{1}{x})$
$= -\frac{1}{7x}(-\log x)^{-\frac{6}{7}}$

Since $-\log x = \log(\frac{1}{x})$, we can substitute back:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{1}{7x (\log(\frac{1}{x}))^{\frac{6}{7}}}$

**Note on the Original Derivation:**
The intermediate steps in the original image show some discrepancies:
1.  The first derivative step introduces a negative sign (`-1/7(...)`) which is not directly from the power rule.
2.  The next step incorrectly changes `d/dx log(1/x)` to `d/dx log x`.
However, the initial incorrect negative sign effectively cancels out the negative sign that would arise from correctly differentiating `log(1/x)` (since `d/dx log(1/x) = d/dx (-log x) = -1/x`), leading to the correct final result.
